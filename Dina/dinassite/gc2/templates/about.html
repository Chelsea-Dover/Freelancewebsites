As an integral coach, I approach every individual client with a curious mind, open perspective, and no pre-determined agenda. Integral coaching includes every aspect of the client and their world - what clients experience now as well as their potential. Through coaching, we explore how clients represent themselves, investigate their social world, habits, their quality of self-care, and the amount of attention and energy available to take on change.

Dina's story

With nearly 20 years of corporate experience to guide me, I am passionate about coaching professionals at various stages of positive transition and change, including career shifts, transitions from leave, and other professional milestones. My own career transitions have included moving cross-country to join Perkins Coie as a lateral labor and employment associate, being the first professional in the Portland office to transition to a reduced hours schedule, leaving the practice of law to become the first Director of Professional Development in the Portland market, and then transitioning to the position I created as an internal career coach. For almost two decades, I have spoken widely on a host of professional development and diversity topics. During this time, I developed and facilitated diversity roundtable discussions with dozens of professionals, creating a safe space to surface sensitive but crucial diversity issues. In my final year at Perkins Coie, I was honored to receive the firm’s inaugural Diversity Champion Award.

"Dina has the ability to see what is needed and figure out how to make it happen."

I have tried to impact diversity more through my actions than my words. I have not felt compelled to be loud or outspoken, just authentic and sincere in my commitment to the work. Diversity work has provided a ripe field for my natural inclinations.

Dina immigrated to the United States from Azerbaijan in 1981 and is an avid world traveler. She also serves on the emeritus board of the National Multiple Sclerosis Society, practices mindfulness and barre3, passionately roots for the Portland Trailblazers, and loves to spend time on Mount Hood with her husband, Darren, 16-year old son, Noah, and mutt, Sabine.
