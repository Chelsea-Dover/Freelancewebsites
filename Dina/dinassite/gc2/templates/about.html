Dina Glassman is a certified integral coach. Integral coaching includes everything about the client and the client’s world. This includes what the client is aware of along with their potential—what could be brought about through focused, skillful methods. Individual coaching explores how clients represent themselves, and also investigates their social world, habits, the quality of self-care, the amount of attention and energy available to take on change, and much more.

"Dina has the ability to see what is needed and figure out how to make it happen."

I have tried to impact diversity more through my actions than my words. I have not felt compelled to be loud or outspoken, just authentic and sincere in my commitment to the work. Diversity work has provided a ripe field for my natural inclinations.

Dina's story

Dina is passionate about coaching attorneys at various stages of positive transition and change, including career shifts, transitions from leave, and other professional milestones. Her own career transitions have included moving cross-country to join Perkins Coie as a lateral labor and employment associate, being the first professional in the Portland office to transition to a reduced hours schedule, leaving the practice of law to become the first Director of Professional Development in the Portland market, and then transitioning to the position she created as an internal career coach. In Dina’s final year at Perkins Coie, she was honored to receive the firm’s inaugural Diversity Champion Award. This year, Dina has gone out on her own to start GC2, her independent coaching and legal talent consulting firm. For almost two decades, Dina has spoken widely on a host of professional development and diversity topics, including the DiSC behavioral assessment, which she has used throughout her career. Dina immigrated to the United States from Azerbaijan in 1981 and is an avid world traveler. She also serves on the emeritus board of the National Multiple Sclerosis Society, practices mindfulness and barre3, passionately roots for the Portland Trailblazers, and loves to spend time on Mount Hood with her husband, Darren, 16-year old son, Noah, and mutt, Sabine.
